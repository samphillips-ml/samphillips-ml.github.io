About

Academic background

I'm an undergraduate at UNC Charlotte, working toward a dual B.S. in Computer Science (Data Science track) and Mathematics (Statistics concentration), alongside an early-entry M.S. in Computer Science. Across these programs I've gravitated toward the same core question from a few different directions: how do we build models that handle data which is sparse, irregular, or evolving over time?

Research identity

My research centers on making hard data problems tractable in sequential and dynamic systems, with representation spaces as my primary tool ‐ I'm interested in what structure a model learns from messy, indirect, or sparse observations, and whether that structure is actually meaningful. So far this has taken shape in ocean and environmental data: modeling Arctic contaminant transport, reconstructing biogeochemical states from sparse Argo float profiles, and (most recently) applying similar ideas to food safety time series. But I think of these as applications of one approach rather than a fixed domain ‐ I'm drawn to problems with this shape wherever they show up, and I'm always interested in new ones.

What's next

I'm currently looking at thesis-based M.S. programs as a next step, with the goal of building out a stronger publication record ahead of a Ph.D. Longer-term, I'm aiming for a research career at a national lab, working on the kinds of sequential and dynamical-systems problems that motivate me now.
