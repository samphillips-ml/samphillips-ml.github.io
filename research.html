Research

My research centers on making sequential and dynamic systems tractable through learned representations — particularly in settings where direct observations are sparse, indirect, or expensive. So far this has meant Arctic contaminant transport, ocean biogeochemistry, and most recently, food safety time series. The application domains vary, but the underlying problem — recovering structure from limited data using representation spaces — stays consistent.

Arctic PFAS Transport Modeling

With Dr. Roger Tipton, UNC Charlotte · NSF Grant #2444939

  • Fall 2025 (OUR Scholar): Configured and ran global-domain MITgcm ocean simulations modeling passive microplastics transport — groundwork in large-scale ocean modeling on HPC infrastructure.
  • Spring 2026: Designed and executed MITgcm LLC270 Arctic Ocean simulations modeling PFAS transport under varying atmospheric forcing conditions. Presented at NCUR 2026.
  • Summer 2026 (in progress): Developing a physics-informed neural network (PINN) framework to correct TOPAZ/CMEMS Arctic simulation outputs against sparse observational PFAS data (~140 samples over 20 years). Targeting AGU Fall 2026.

Representation Learning for Biogeochemical State Reconstruction

With Dr. Xuyang Li, UNC Charlotte · OUR Scholar

  • Spring 2026: Initial work modeled latent ocean dynamics from Argo float profiles using neural ODEs. This outperformed simple baselines but not domain-specific architectures — informing the approach below.
  • Summer 2026 (in progress): Developing a transformer-based architecture for biogeochemical state reconstruction from sparse float observations, currently outperforming the convolutional benchmark used in prior state-of-the-art work. Targeting Geoscientific Model Development (GMD).

Neural Time Series Modeling for Food Safety Prediction

NSF REU, Worcester Polytechnic Institute · Advisor: Dr. Fabricio Murai

  • Summer 2026 (in progress): Developing and evaluating deep learning approaches — including time series foundation models and multimodal architectures — to predict and explain bacterial growth, contamination, and spoilage in food systems. Targeting MIT's Undergraduate Research Conference.

Representation Geometry in Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning

With Jake Minwu Lee, UNC Charlotte

  • Summer 2026 (early stage): Surveying representation geometry in multi-agent reinforcement learning systems, building toward a proposal for UNC Charlotte's undergraduate research grant program.

I'm domain-flexible — the throughline across these is making sequential and dynamic data tractable through learned representations, not the particular domain it shows up in.
